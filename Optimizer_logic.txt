## Layer 2 — The Judge

**Job:** Of the 25–53 stocks that passed Layer 1, rank them from best to worst. Give each a score.

Five ingredients, combined into one number:

```
EMA50%  × 33%   How far above EMA50 is the price? Sweet spot 8-30%.
                 MTARTECH was +33% → high score → selected → gained +54.4%

Tech    × 23%   Technical score from RSI, ADX, MACD, EMA alignment

ML Conf × 20%   How confident is the ML model? 73% minimum already passed
                 Layer 1. Here higher conf gets more points.

Ranks   × 20%   SecRnk + CapRnk — how good is this stock within its
                 sector and cap category?

F25d    × 4%    25-day forecast — only a tiebreaker. You were right
                 not to trust it primarily. INDSWFTLAB had highest F25d
                 (+10.5%) but actually lost -2%.

Then multiply the whole score by sector strength:
  Strong Uptrend ↑↑ sector → score × 1.3  (30% bonus)
  Plain Uptrend ↑ sector   → score × 1.0  (no change)
```

Output: a ranked list. GMDCLTD scores 90.7. TORNTPOWER scores 93.8. Top 15 go to Layer 3.

## Layer 3 — The Accountant

**Job:** Decide how much money to put in each stock. Make sure we do not over-concentrate anywhere.

Uses **equal allocation** — confirmed by your test data. Rank weighted vs equal made only 0.28% difference. Stock selection matters 30x more than allocation method.

```
Allocation per stock = Capital ÷ number of qualifying stocks

Example: Rs 2,00,000 capital, 10 stocks qualify
         → Rs 20,000 per stock

Then apply three caps:

Cap 1 — Per stock: never more than 15% in one stock
         Rs 2,00,000 × 15% = Rs 30,000 maximum per stock

Cap 2 — Per sector: dynamic based on how many sectors qualify
         If 6 sectors qualify → each sector gets up to 25%
         If 2 sectors qualify → each sector gets up to 50%
         If 1 sector qualifies → maximum 60% (absolute ceiling)
         
         This is important for bear markets when only metals or
         pharma is holding up — you should concentrate there,
         not force diversification into weak sectors.

Cap 3 — Total: never exceed 100% of capital
```

Also computes and shows the **ATR stop** for each stock — not as an exit trigger but as a reference. Exit is decided by indicators, not by a price level.

## Layer 4 — The Doctor

**Job:** Check if the stock is healthy right now. A stock can pass Layers 1–3 perfectly but still be in the middle of a pullback from a recent peak. Layer 4 catches this.

This is what catches the **Torrent Power problem** — price is +15% above EMA50 (passes Layer 1) but the stock peaked at +25% two weeks ago and is now sliding down. Layer 1 cannot see this. Layer 4 can.

Five signals, each scores +1 (healthy) or -1 (problem):

```
Signal 1 — 5-day momentum
  Is price higher than 5 days ago?
  Yes → +1   No → -1
  
Signal 2 — Position in recent range
  Is price closing near its recent highs or near its recent lows?
  Near highs (top 60% of 5-day range) → +1  ← RECOVERING stock scores well here
  Near lows  (bottom 40% of range)    → -1  ← TORNTPOWER scores -1 here
  
  TORNTPOWER: High=1790, Low=1675, Close=1699
  Position = (1699-1675)/(1790-1675) = 0.21 → closing near lows → -1

Signal 3 — EMA20 slope
  Is the short-term trend (EMA20) still rising?
  Rising → +1   Flattening or falling → -1

Signal 4 — RSI direction
  Is RSI rising while below 75? → momentum building → +1
  Is RSI falling while above 65? → momentum fading at highs → -1

Signal 5 — Higher low and higher close (RECOVERY BONUS)
  Is today's low higher than 5 days ago's low?
  AND is today's close higher than 5 days ago's close?
  Both yes → +1 bonus (stock retraced and is now resuming uptrend)
  
  This is the signal that CAPTURES a stock that retracted
  to 8% above EMA50, turned up, and is now at 10% above EMA50.
  Signal 2 would show it closing near highs. Signal 5 gives it a bonus.
  These stocks are often the best entries — weak holders cleared out,
  RSI reset, ready for next leg up.
```

Total score -4 to +5:

```
+4 or +5  →  ✅ RISING or RECOVERING    Clean entry, full allocation
+1 to +3  →  ⚠️ MIXED                  Show with caution label
   0      →  ⚠️ NEUTRAL                Consolidating, not ideal timing
-1 to -4  →  🚫 RETREATING             Peaked and falling — exclude
```

```
  🚫 RETREATING      → skip for now, re-evaluate next run
```
